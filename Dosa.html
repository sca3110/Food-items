Dosa

ingredients

  • Rice
  • Urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • Fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
  • Salt
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately in enough water for at least 4-6 hours, or preferably overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked ingredients and rinse them thoroughly.
  3. In a blender or wet grinder, grind the soaked rice and urad dal separately to a smooth paste, adding water gradually as needed. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter.
  4. Combine the ground rice and urad dal batter in a large bowl. Add salt to taste and mix well.
  5. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth or lid and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight. The batter should rise and become slightly fluffy.
  6. After fermentation, gently stir the batter. If it is too thick, add a little water to adjust the consistency to a pourable batter consistency.
  7. Heat a non-stick or cast iron griddle (tava) over medium-high heat.
  8. Once the griddle is hot, pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the griddle and quickly spread it in a circular motion to form a thin, even layer (similar to a crepe).
  9. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa and cook for 1-2 minutes, or until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy.
  10. Flip the dosa using a spatula and cook the other side for another 1-2 minutes, or until lightly browned and crisp.
  11. Remove the dosa from the griddle and serve hot with chutney and sambar.
  12. Repeat the process with the remaining batter, stirring the batter gently before making each dosa.
  13. Enjoy your homemade dosas!
